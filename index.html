Bio

I am a Master's student in Mathematical Biology at The University of British Columbia, working with Eric Cytrynbaum. Previously, I worked with Matthew McTammany in biology at Bucknell University. I study biological systems modeling, and I enjoy sharing my passion for mathematics through teaching. When I’m not thinking about math or teaching, I’m reading, swimming, painting or walking along the beach.

Research

The University of British Columbia

I am currently working on lactation and infant metabolism. This project involves generating a model that uses neurological, hormonal and external stimuli to describe human milk production during lactation in order to undrestand how milk production increases. Additionally, a model to describe infant growth based on metabolism and milk intake is being generated.

This work will be presented at:

  1. Mathematical Biology Seminar, 2019

Bucknell University

During my undergraduate degree, I did my Honor's Thesis work on estimating river metabolism. In this project, I used dissolved oxygen data to estimated daily ecosystem metabolism rates for the Susquehanna River in order to determine the effect of the river ecosystem on atmospheric carbon dioxide.

This work was presented at:

  1. The NIMBioS Undergraduate Research Conference, 2016
  2. The Susquehanna River Symposium, 2016
  3. The Kalman Research Symposium, 2017

The abstract for this thesis can be found here:
Faulkner, Katharine R. "Estimation of daily net ecosystem production rates in the Susquehanna River using inverse modeling with dissolved oxygen data." Honors Thesis, Bucknell University. (2017).
